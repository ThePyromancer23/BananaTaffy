The Road So Far

Gay Furry Highschool was originally devised as a joke. Since then, it evolved into what is undoubtedly one of my proudest efforts-- the start of a highly configurable and unique dating sim. I have put way too many hours into this project, primarily in the spring of 2019, but over the course of the last two years I have added systems, performed bug fixes, and wrote several prose storylines for the various characters. While I had never intended this project to get this far, I would like to push it even further. The following is what has currently been done for this project... feel free to sigh and shake your head, or look on in bewilderment.


Story and Characters

Much of the overarching GFHS story has been penciled in: there is a cult surrounding the school that can account for many of the strange happenings, and even what is going on with the concerning principal: Mr. Moseby. Most of the individual character stories directly relate the happenings in the town: you may get bits and pieces of the full story on each route but will be able to connect all of the ways the cult is affecting people, and exactl what they are up to by playing through each route. None of these furthered stories have been implemented into the game itself as of yet, just penciled in and subject to change as it is decided exactly where we want to take the story of Gay Furry Highschool.


Current Character Roster

These are the characters that are currently partially implemented into the game. This roster makes up the "core" characters of GFHS, more on that later.


Mr. Moseby: The principal and dastardly cult leader of GFHS. While I am unsure how to implement his own route, he is crazed, fanatical, and obsessed with vore. He provides a stereotypical yandere archetype and under most conditions can be seen as an antagonist, with a few routes that are meant just to spite him and of course stop him and his cult.


Mackai the Bear: This character is the standard for GFHS. The 'childhood friend' equivalent of other dating sims. Mackai is slow to anger, and will help the player acclimate to the school, occasionally introduce the player to other potential love interests, and provide... interesting insight into various topics. Mackai is homeless and tends to run into issues with the cult on the streets, but it's hard to get him to open up about such topics.


Andre the Fox: Andre is a more difficult character to woo, for which routes may have him become more of an antagonistic figure towards the player. Andre is a reclusive and uptight nerd who's family wants nothing to do with him. He cares little for school and spends most of his time working on cases with the police department for his "internship". Namely, he is trying to uncover evidence of the secret cult that has been plaguing the town.


Riley the Wolf: Riley is a jock, that is clear from the moment the player meets this character, but that may not be his most prominent trait. He's not bright, but he's trying hard, and he's very kind. This character is more of a challenge than Mackai but generally provides an agreeable character. Riley has a dark secret though-- He draws adult comics. When the cult finds out about his artistic nature, he is blackmailed into doing odd things.

Max the German Shephard: Max is a gamer guy. With an upbeat attitude and a love for games, Max struggles through the story as he gets closer to player... and finds many issues with the relationship he is already in. The player may choose to help him resolve his issues, steal Max outright, or even enable Max to cheat on his partner. Max's route could be wholesome or very scandalous, depending on the moral judgements made throughout.


Ace the Ferret: Ace is the resident bad boy of GFHS... and he will show very little interest in the player until he starts breaking Moseby's rules and getting detentions. Ace is a difficult character woo simply because he will only be found where criminal activity is also found, and will directly contradict Moseby's rules, making it very dangerous to pursue Ace.


Several other characters have been planned but not added to the game in any capacity. These will be touched on later in the roadmap.

Interface

The interface for GFHS is all rainbow-themed. Headache inducingly rainbow-themed. This is something that is intended to change in the future, but for the time being it provides a colorful thematic foreground for our game. The UI is fairly self-explanatory, with menus for the players stats, relationships, money, current day, and when needed (and enabled) tutorials. While functional, better text boxes and menus should be added. (Ignore the Dbg button in the picture, that's for debugging purposes!)


Game Systems

There are several systems that make up the game currently, these are what elevate GFHS from a standard visual novel with branching choices to a real gaming experience.


  • Relationships: It's not just the important choices that make up a characters relationship with the player. Even small interactions slowly build relationships and allow for more stories to unlock.
  • Stats and Challenges: At various points in the game the player will be tested on the stats that they have grown and cultivated. Not just for looks, these stats can get you out of trouble and further stories.
  • Random Events: Not every day is the same: Each section of the day provides opportunities to interact with characters or many other things. Events can fire from walking to school, hanging out before school, morning and afternoon classes, and afterschool activities.
  • Lunch: Lunch is the players main opportunity to take control of who they run into, by letting them spend lunch time raising their relationship with a character of their choice, often providing unique stories.

The Present

Now that we've looked at the current state of the game... what is currently being done for Gay Furry Highschool. Most notably, writing and bug fixing. For Riley and Andre, their unique routes have been started in an attempt to get closer to having a workable demo finished. In order to prepare for GFHS to become a full game, alterations have made to the code to make it more organized and streamlined. Additionally, BUG FIXES. SO MANY BUG FIXES. In it's current state, GFHS is playable, with missing features and missing stories. On the plus side, the framework is in place to tell wonderful stories and make something real.


The following 'chapters' of this roadmap explain what is missing from the current game, what I want to change, what I want to add, and everything else. Everything on this site is in the order that things should potentially be completed in, but everything is heavily subject to change, based on feedback and anything else that might come up. I refuse to set anything in stone at this stage and would like as much help as possible coming up with ideas and alterations that can make GFHS... genuinely a good game (despite the fact that this was supposed to be a joke.)


Complete Classes Demo

Even at the early stages, I want to be able to present something properly playable, and something that we can get excited about and say "hey, that's a game!" Currently, we almost have that, but a few more things would need to be done before we could have something that could be called a demo. While still missing several core features, I would like a demo to include a week of gameplay, subtracting the afterschool, dating, and gifting systems.


Demo To-Do List

  • Proper Relationship Planning: Each choice and relationship is a numbers game... but these numbers do not properly mean anything or reflect any larger scope of the game. Before proceeding much further, each starting and ending point needs to be analyzed so we can see what it will look like for players to move up and down the ranks of this system, without going too fast or too slow.
  • Adequate Storyline Completion: Once we know how long it should take to progress through the relationships, we can decide how many stories on each branch would be able to fit into a week, at maximum. Since events are random, we have to assume that a player could interact with any given character the maximum possible number of times to make sure nothing is missing.
  • Graphics: It would be idea to have each necessary character graphic implemented before publishing a demo, this isn't completely necessary however.
  • Clean Up: To ensure the game is playable and the passage of time throughout the game is understandable, more clear transitions should be implemented. Making the interface more visually pleasing and bug fixing can help produce a polished look.

What will this look like?

At this stage, Gay Furry Highschool will have a full week in which you can interact with characters and progress through their stories in what will probably end up being a very linear fashion. Occasional challenges may occur, and the afterschool portion of the game will only be used for any necessary detentions. When doing general interactions, no options for gifting, phone numbers, or dates will be visible.


Clubs, Gifts, Dates, Work

Expanding upon the general dating sim style, GFHS is intended to feature afterschool clubs, the ability to buy and give gifts to characters, the ability to take characters out on dates, and some sort of minigame for working on Sundays to earn extra money. These options can help raise stats, progress stories, see unique stories, and progress relationships.


Not very many things have been decided upon for these systems. The general idea is that players will be able to choose certain clubs on certain days during the after school portion of the game. This will allow them to potentially meet new characters, raise stats by practicing activities, or interact with characters they've already met. If the player has landed detention, they will serve detention during this time. If the player has invited anyone on a date that day, then that will occur, and if nothing else, they can go home for private time to raise their sex stat or go purchase gifts. The after school portion of the game will house almost all of the currently missing systems. Options for gifts and dates will occur when talking to characters during the day, and the work minigame will take place every Sunday, when players do not have classes. This breaks up the Visual Novel style slightly.


Full Character Stories

At this point, the next thing that will need to be focused on is finishing the route for each of the "core" characters. This will include options for clubs, dates, general interactions, following their stories start to finish. Writing these stories will most likely be one of the largest undertakings of GFHS. Sex scenes are still... up for debate at this point in time.


Non-"Base" Characters

In addition to the default characters that have been implemented already and are mentioned above, the intention is to take GFHS even further with more character options. These characters may be more difficult to meet than the "core" characters, or meeting a certain character might lock the player out of meeting another. It would be good to avoid too many characters being unlocked at once so the player has a decent change to talk to desirable characters on any given day. That, or an option will need to be implemented that will allow players to avoid one or two characters.


Additional Planned Characters:


Jake the Sloth: Jake is a pothead to put it gently. He's very chill and friendly, but he hates his drug habit and wants to drop it. He only started smoking because his brother got him into it, but it's caused some weird hallucinations at times.


Diego the Cat: Diego thinks negatively of the world. He dresses in black, despite the dress code, and only listens to music he'd describe as 'punk'. To further his rebellion from his sweet, loving family... he joined Mr. Moseby's evil cult.


Steve the Rabbit: Steve is not the best-looking in school, but has a good heart. Of course, Steve is disguised because he is actually a celebrity, but no one needs to know that... except the crazy stalker that is determined to get close to him.


Thomas the Goat: Thomas is a quiet and artistic character, and will not open up to the player very easily. He is haunted by the ghost of his father, who was a famous painter.


John Titor the Synth: John Titor is a secret character that will only be unlocked by getting detention for breaking all four of Mr. Moseby's rules. Titor is time traveler who was sent back to destroy Moseby's cult.


What Does It Look Like Finished?

Ideally, a finished Gay Furry Highschool is a game where you can go to school to meet a large variety of characters, replay the game to see different routes, experience the game differently each time through choices and random events, raise stats, strategize how to run into favorite characters more often, or what dates and gifts they might like... all of these things as well as stuff like, pissing off characters you don't like, or uncovering different elements of the cult. Overall, this still isn't meant to be a completely serious project, but it could be a genuinely good game. A genuinely good dating sim, even. But more than anything I think a finished product for GFHS is something that will produce a few laughs.


I wrote this whole roadmap to illustrate for everyone interested in where we've been, where we are, and where I would personally like to go. There's so much undecided and so much subject to change but I hope I produced a clear picture of all of this and maybe even made you excited for the possiblities. It's not a small undertaking, but if there are people willing to help, pitch ideas, write, playtest... it will become a much more reasonable goal. I know this project was largely abandoned quite a while ago now, but it's never something that has left my peripherals as a writer or as a programmer. If people are no longer interested because of the gap or for any other reason, I understand, but at the same time, if there are those willing to give this a chance with me. Thank you, and please let me know.
